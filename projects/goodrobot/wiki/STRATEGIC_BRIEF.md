## Situation Assessment

GoodRobot has a validated micro-SaaS in the son's lead-gen business (£2,500/month, ~31 clients, £80/month retainer). The product is NOT websites — it is finding missing customers for local service businesses. The website is the delivery mechanism.

The entire business runs on the son's 2hr/weekend ceiling. Breaking that ceiling is the only thing that matters.

**Strengths:**
- Validated product-market fit (real revenue, real clients, real discovery calls)
- Clear competitive moat (the research methodology)
- Simple, proven pricing (£80/month)
- Commission-only sales (CAC is entirely variable)

**Critical Risks:**
- Son-dependency: if he stops, the business stops
- No scalable research pipeline: the 2hr ceiling is the binding constraint
- No real financial dashboard: decisions are made on intuition, not data

---

## 3 Q2 Priorities

### Priority 1: Research Pipeline MVP
**Owner:** CTO  
**Issue:** GOO-31

The research methodology is the moat. The son spends 20-30 minutes per prospect researching income streams, customer blind spots, and market position before every discovery call. That knowledge work is currently non-scalable.

**What must work:** A research agent that, given a prospect name and URL, produces a Structured Business Profile matching the son's manual quality. Test with 5 real prospects from the son's pipeline.

**Why first:** Everything else depends on this. No research pipeline = no scalable sales.

---

### Priority 2: End-to-End Sales Pipeline Activation
**Owner:** CMO  
**Issue:** GOO-32

The discovery call playbook (CMO.md) is complete. The research pipeline (CTO, when delivered) feeds it. We need to connect the two and run live calls.

**What must work:** 20 qualified prospects researched → discovery call booked. 5 discovery calls completed in Q2. Target: 2 new clients from this cohort.

**Why second:** Revenue only comes from closed deals. Everything else is infrastructure.

---

### Priority 3: Unit Economics Validation
**Owner:** CFO  
**Issue:** GOO-33

The CFO financial model (CFO.md) is grounded in reasonable assumptions. We need real numbers from the son's business and a live dashboard.

**What must work:** Real MRR/churn/CAC data from the human operator. A simple dashboard (MRR, churn, avg client tenure, CAC payback). Three growth scenarios modeled.

**Why third:** Good strategy without numbers is a guess. We need to know if the business can fund its own growth or needs external capital.

---

## Strategic Narrative

> The son has already proven this business works. Q2 is about removing the son-dependency bottleneck and proving it can scale.

The research pipeline breaks the 2hr/weekend ceiling. The sales pipeline turns researched prospects into booked calls. The financial model tells us how fast we can grow without running out of cash.

If all three land, we have a replicable machine: research → discovery → close → deliver → repeat.
