## 1. Target Customer Profile

### Primary Persona: Operations-Overwhelmed SMB Owner

- **Company size**: 10–200 employees
- **Industries**: Professional services, healthcare administration, retail, financial services
- **Pain points**:
  - Repetitive operational tasks consuming owner/manager time
  - No engineering staff to build custom AI integrations
  - Existing "toys" (basic chatbots, simple automation) underdelivering
  - Enterprise AI platforms priced and scoped for companies 10× their size
- **Decision-makers**: Owner, COO, Operations Director, Office Manager
- **Geography**: English-speaking markets (US, Canada, UK, Australia) initially
- **Budget authority**: $300–$2,500/month for operational improvements
- **Technology comfort**: Moderate — uses Slack, Zoom, Zapier; has been burned by overpromised AI before

### Secondary Persona: Solo Entrepreneur / Freelancer

- 1–5 person operation needing team-scale automation
- Pain: wearing every hat, drowning in admin work
- Willing to pay Starter tier ($299/mo) for 3 agents
- Values speed of setup over advanced customization

### Customer Disqualifiers

- Companies under 5 employees with no visible operational complexity
- Enterprises requiring SOC2, HIPAA, or custom compliance (not in v1)
- Prospects already deeply invested in Salesforce/MS Copilot ecosystems
- Companies with no digital workflows (all paper/phone-based)
- Businesses in non-English speaking markets (v1 only)

## 4. Pricing Page Strategy

### Tier Architecture Mapped to Segments

| Tier | Price | Buyer | Job-to-be-Done |
|------|-------|-------|----------------|
| **Starter** | $299/mo | Solo / micro-SMB | "I want to dip my toe in AI without risk" |
| **Professional** | $899/mo | Growing SMB | "I need real automation that scales with me" |
| **Enterprise** | $2,499/mo | Operations leader | "I need SLA, SSO, and someone to hold my hand" |

### Pricing Page Principles

1. **Anchor Enterprise**: Show $2,499 first (top-left), then let Professional and Starter feel like bargains
2. **Show the math**: "For $299/mo, recover 10 hours/week at $30/hr = $4,800/mo value"
3. **No pricing surprises**: Include agent limits, task limits, and overage costs upfront
4. **Annual discount**: 2 months free (17% off) — primary ask, drive LTV
5. **Social proof beside each tier**: "Most popular for [segment]" with a real or representative company type

### Overage Strategy

- Starter: hard cap at 1,000 tasks, no overage (upsell trigger)
- Professional: $0.002/call overage — predictable if usage spikes
- Enterprise: unlimited tasks — no friction for high-volume customers

## 7. Sales Motion: SMB Self-Serve vs. Enterprise Assisted

### SMB Self-Serve (Starter / Professional)

**Trigger**: Trial sign-up, pricing page visit, or inbound content download

**Motion**:
1. Trial starts → automated onboarding email sequence (Day 1, 3, 7, 10)
2. In-app prompts at key activation moments (first agent configured, first task completed)
3. If no upgrade by Day 7 → human touchpoint: "Did you get stuck?" email from sales@goodrobot.com
4. If trial expiring → "Last chance" email with link to talk to human
5. Annual upsell at renewal: "Switch to annual, save 2 months"

**Self-serve disqualifiers** (route to human):
- 5+ agent requests
- Custom integration asks
- Enterprise features (SSO, audit logs) in scope
- Enterprise-tier pricing expectation

### Enterprise Assisted (Professional / Enterprise)

**Trigger**: Company size > 50 employees, 5+ agents needed, custom ask, inbound demo request

**Motion**:
1. Demo request → 30-min discovery call within 24 hours
2. Discovery call agenda:
   - Understand current operational pain (use the son's discovery framework)
   - Map specific workflows to GoodRobot agents
   - Give a realistic preview of 30-day outcomes
   - Quote appropriate tier + professional services if needed
3. Proposal / demo environment within 48 hours of call
4. Trial extension offered (2 weeks) with success criteria defined
5. Close: 30-day pilot at Professional + onboarding fee → convert to annual

### Human-Qualify Trigger Points

Route to human sales when:
- Prospect asks for custom roles
- Company has 50+ employees
- Prospect mentions compliance requirements
- Prospect wants to speak to references
- Trial usage suggests complex multi-agent deployment
- Any mention of competitor evaluation (they're in buying mode)

## 8. Retention and Expansion

### Retention Drivers (keep churn < 5%/month)

| Driver | Tactic | Frequency |
|--------|--------|----------|
| **Activation** | Onboarding sequence, first-task success | Day 1–7 |
| **ROI visibility** | Monthly usage report emailed ("You automated X tasks, saved Y hours") | Monthly |
| **Proactive support** | "Is everything working?" check-in at Day 30, 60, 90 | Quarterly |
| **New agent releases** | "New role available: [name] — would this help?" | Monthly |
| **Annual lock-in** | Offer 2 months free on annual | At renewal |

### Expansion (LTV growth: $650 → $1,200+ ARPU)

| Expansion Trigger | Tactic | Expected Lift |
|-------------------|--------|---------------|
| Usage near task limit | "You're at 90% of your task limit — here's what Professional unlocks" | $299 → $899 |
| 3+ agents in use | "Your 4th agent is included in Professional — want me to add it?" | $299 → $899 |
| Cross-department usage | Introduce second team (e.g., marketing after sales success) | $899 → $1,798 |
| Enterprise features needed | SSO / audit log conversation | $899 → $2,499 |
| Annual commitment | Switch to annual, save $3,588/yr | Churn → 12-month LTV |

### Churn Prevention

- Flag accounts with usage drop > 30% month-over-month (risk signal)
- Flag accounts that haven't configured a new agent in 30+ days (activation stall)
- CEO / senior outreach for at-risk Enterprise accounts

## 10. Competitor Positioning

### Positioning Matrix

| Competitor | Claim they make | Reality | GoodRobot counter |
|------------|----------------|---------|-------------------|
| DIY tools (Make/Zapier + ChatGPT) | "Build your own AI workflow" | Requires tech skills, no domain expertise, maintenance burden | "We build the agents. You just approve what they do." |
| Generic chatbots (Intercom Fin, etc.) | "AI customer support" | One-size-fits-all, no business learning | "Agents trained on YOUR business, not generic FAQs" |
| Enterprise (Salesforce Einstein, MS Copilot) | "AI at enterprise scale" | 6-month implementation, $100K+ contracts, overkill for SMB | "SMB pricing. Live in a week. Priced for companies like yours." |
| Agency / consultant builds | "Custom AI built for you" | $50K–$200K, 3–6 months, single-use | "Pre-built agents, ready today, for $299/month" |

### How to Win in a Competitive Evaluation

When a prospect says "We're also looking at [competitor]":

1. **Acknowledge the competitor's strength** — never dismiss
2. **Reframe the comparison**: "Those are built for [different use case] — let me show you what we see when we talk to companies like yours"
3. **Focus on time-to-value**: "When did [competitor] last close a deal because of 30-day ROI evidence?"
4. **Lean into SMB-specific signals**: "What matters to a company your size isn't the most features — it's whoever gets you results fastest"
5. **Create urgency**: "We have a beta pricing lock for companies that move in the next 30 days"
