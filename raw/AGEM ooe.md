| **Novelty** | Unlimited within bounds | Unlimited including representational |

### The Four Axioms of Open-Endedness

1. **Unbounded Novelty Axiom**: Infinite sequence of non-derivable novel behaviors
2. **Complexification Axiom**: Increasing hierarchical organization over time
3. **Major Transition Condition**: Capacity for new entity types (not just instances)
4. **Autonomy Axiom**: Self-referential modification of search space and criteria

## 🎯 Key Insight

> **The paradox of open-endedness**: To achieve it, a system must be capable of outgrowing its own description—creating new representational languages and selective criteria. This creates bootstrapping problems reminiscent of Gödel's incompleteness. Natural evolution succeeded because it was never constrained by a fixed description—it could literally instantiate new physical substrates (DNA, nervous systems, culture) as it evolved.

**AGEM itself demonstrates partial open-endedness** through its evolving conceptual complexity across cycles, but within a fixed representational framework. True strong OEE would require architectural innovations that remain active research frontiers.
