### The Illusion of AI "Theory of Mind"

Because AI can communicate fluently, humans often project a "self" onto it—an inherent tendency to apply folk psychology to artificial agents. We assume that because an AI can discuss its "thoughts," it must possess a mind.

Recent studies show that Large Language Models (LLMs) can achieve human-level performance on classic Theory of Mind (ToM) reasoning tasks, mimicking the ability to understand beliefs and intentions. However, this mimicry is structurally fragile. Trivial changes to a prompt can cause the AI to fail completely, proving that current AI success relies on sophisticated pattern recognition and statistical inference, not the genuine, flexible mental state representations found in human cognition. AI operates through functional mimicry, not actual subjective experience.
