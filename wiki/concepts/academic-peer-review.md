# Academic Peer Review

Academic peer review is the "gatekeeping" process of science. It involves the evaluation of research by independent experts (peers) to ensure it meets the standards of the discipline before it is published in a journal or presented at a conference.

## Purpose
- **Quality Control**: Filters out work with flawed methodology or insufficient evidence.
- **Validation**: Confirms that the results are credible and the interpretations are reasonable.
- **Improvement**: Provides feedback to authors to refine their work.

## Types of Review
- **Single-blind**: Reviewers know the authors' identities, but authors do not know the reviewers'.
- **Double-blind**: Neither the authors nor the reviewers know each other's identities.
- **Open review**: All identities are known.
