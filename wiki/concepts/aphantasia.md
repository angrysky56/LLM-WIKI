# Aphantasia

**Aphantasia** is a neurological condition where individuals are unable to visualize mental images. It affects approximately 1% of the population.

## Neurological Basis

Recent research, particularly associated with the [[spontaneous-activity-reshaping-hypothesis]], suggests that aphantasia may be linked to:
- **High Excitability:** Neurons in the early visual cortex of aphantasics fire more readily on their own.
- **Resilience to Shaping:** Because the spontaneous patterns are so active and "noisy," they are harder to hold in shape or "carve" into recognizable images using top-down suppression.

## Spectrum of Visualization

- **Aphantasia:** Zero or near-zero mental imagery (1 in 100).
- **Hyperphantasia:** Extremely vivid, near-perceptual mental imagery (1 in 30).
