# Broca's Area

**Broca's area** is a region in the posterior inferior frontal gyrus of the dominant hemisphere (typically the left) of the brain. It is primarily associated with the production of coherent speech and the coordination of the muscle movements required for articulation.

## Key Functions
- **Speech Production**: Planning and execution of motor movements for the lips, tongue, and larynx.
- **Syntax and Grammar**: Assembling words into grammatically correct structures.
- **Working Memory**: Holding language-based information during sentence formulation.

## Clinical Significance: Broca's Aphasia
Damage to this area (e.g., via stroke) results in **Expressive Aphasia**:
- **Symptoms**: Labored, halting speech; "telegraphic" sentences (omission of function words).
- **Preserved Ability**: Language comprehension usually remains intact.
- **Awareness**: Patients are typically aware of their deficit, leading to significant frustration.
