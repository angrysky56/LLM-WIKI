# Critical Analysis

Critical analysis in a scientific context is the objective and systematic evaluation of a research study's claims, methods, and results. It moves beyond mere summary to assess the internal consistency, external validity, and broader impact of the work.

## Key Dimensions

### 1. Epistemic Quality
Assessing whether the conclusions are justified by the evidence presented. This involves checking for:
- **Logical Coherence**: Do the results support the hypothesis?
- **Methodological Rigor**: Are the chosen techniques appropriate for the research question?
- **Interpretative Accuracy**: Does the discussion stay grounded in the data, or are the claims overextended?

### 2. Contextual Situating
Understanding where the work fits in the history of the field.
- **Foundational Knowledge**: What prior work does this build upon?
- **Scientific Disruption**: Does the work refine existing ideas or present a paradigm shift? (See [[edm-framework]])

### 3. Practical and Theoretical Significance
Evaluating the "so what?" of the research.
- **Novelty**: Does it provide new information or tools?
- **Applicability**: Are there social, technological, or medical implications?
