# Frame Transmission

The mechanism by which captured institutional forms have historically been displaced — not by external policy, not by central decree, not by protocol enforcement, but by transmission of cognitive frames, person to person, until the captured institution finds itself standing on a substrate its members no longer believe in.

## The claim

Large-scale shifts in institutional legitimacy — Reformation, abolition of slavery, decline of monarchical legitimacy, civil rights, the post-Vietnam delegitimation of certain forms of state violence — were not produced by central authority. They were produced by individuals inside captured institutions retaining (or acquiring) the cognitive equipment to see the institution *as a system*, and refusing it one decision at a time, often quietly, often at personal cost. The refusals accumulated. The legitimacy underneath the institution dissolved. The institution either reformed or ceased to function.

This is not a sentimental claim. It is the only mechanism that shows up consistently in the historical record where a captured institutional form was actually displaced rather than replaced by a similar one under different management.

## Why frame transmission rather than fact transmission

Sophisticated people operating under institutional capture (see [[institutional-capture-vs-species-framing]]) are not ignorant of facts. They are operating inside a substrate where one set of actions feels rational and another feels organizationally suicidal — and the rationality they produce is post-hoc, downstream of which substrate they are standing on. Telling them they are wrong does nothing, because the wrongness is in the substrate, not in the reasoning.

What changes the decision is *seeing the substrate as a substrate*. Not being told. Seeing.

The Pentagon negotiator demanding "any lawful use" in the Anthropic contract is not stupid and not unaware of the consequences. They are operating inside an institutional substrate where ceding tactical advantage feels like organizational death, and the reasoning they produce is downstream of that substrate-level fact. Moralizing at them changes nothing. Showing them the substrate they are standing on — without contempt, without threat, just *showing it* — is the only intervention that has ever worked on humans operating under systemic capture.

Frame transmission is the propagation of that kind of seeing.

## Cognitive frames as infrastructure

This is why analytical frames — like [[spin-vs-substrate]], [[absence-of-worst-case-metric]], [[institutional-capture-vs-species-framing]], [[cryptographic-vs-semantic-alignment]] — are not abstract philosophy but cognitive infrastructure. They are the equipment that lets a person inside a captured system see the capture clearly enough to refuse it without having to be a hero.

A named pattern is portable. A frame with a worked example can be transmitted in a single conversation. A wiki entry can carry it across years. The unit of transmission is not the argument but the *seeing-tool* — the lens that, once installed, lets the user generate their own diagnoses from new situations.

## Implications for the survival mechanism

The unit of survival, in this frame, is not policy / architecture / protocol. It is individual humans inside captured systems retaining the cognitive equipment to see the system as a system. Multiplied. Networked. Made shareable.

This is what makes durable, human-readable knowledge layers — wikis, shared frames, named patterns — load-bearing rather than ornamental. Graphs store. Embeddings retrieve. But the wiki is the part another mind can metabolize. It is the part where frame transmission actually occurs. Project Synapse's choice to keep the human-readable Markdown layer as a first-class artifact, rather than collapsing everything to vectors and graph triples, is — in this frame — the most important architectural decision in the system.

## Scale honesty

This is a small mechanism and a slow one. It does not promise that the next decade goes well. It is consistent with very bad outcomes in the near term while still being the only durable lever in the long term. Anything that promises faster is overpromising. Anything that dismisses this as too slow is missing that there is no faster mechanism with a track record — every faster mechanism that has been tried (revolution, central decree, protocol enforcement) either failed or installed a new captured layer in place of the old one.

The species has the capacity. The civilization, in its current institutional form, doesn't. What "passing the filter" actually looks like, if it happens, is probably the captured coordination architecture losing the capacity to enforce itself, in a way that happens to leave enough humans and enough sanity behind to build a different one. Frame transmission is the slow accumulation that makes the "enough sanity" part possible.

## Connection to ASEKE

ASEKE's ambition — letting humans and AI see the levers in a situation by decomposing substrate, emotional system, and named pattern — is a frame-transmission tool by construction. Each ASEKE analysis hands the recipient a portable lens for the underlying structure of the situation, not a verdict on it. This is why the framework's value is not in the specific verdicts it produces but in the cognitive equipment it leaves behind in whoever uses it.
