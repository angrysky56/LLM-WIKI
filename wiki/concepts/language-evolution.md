# Language Evolution

Language evolution refers to the processes by which human languages change in structure, vocabulary, and semantic representation over historical and evolutionary timescales.

## Key Statistical Laws
- **Frequency Assortativity**: The tendency for words of similar frequency (popularity) to cluster semantically.
- **Taylor's Law**: A power-law relationship between the mean and variance of entity counts in space or time.
- **Punctuational Bursts**: Rapid shifts in vocabulary or meaning often correlated with cultural or technological changes.

## Generative Models
- **Directed Preferential Placement (DPP)**: A model where new concepts are added to semantic space based on existing density and proximity to recent innovations.

## AI Implications
- **Emergent Communication**: As LLMs evolve, they may develop native communication protocols that optimize latent space representation beyond human low-bandwidth constraints.
- **Semantic Hot Spots**: Identifying regions of rapid evolution can guide efficient model updating.
