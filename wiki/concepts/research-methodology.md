# Research Methodology

Research methodology refers to the systematic, theoretical analysis of the methods applied to a field of study. It comprises the theoretical analysis of the body of methods and principles associated with a branch of knowledge.

## Components
- **Research Design**: The overall strategy for the study (e.g., experimental, correlational, descriptive).
- **Data Collection**: The tools and techniques used to gather information (e.g., surveys, interviews, observations, measurements).
- **Data Analysis**: The methods used to interpret the collected data (e.g., statistical tests, thematic analysis).

## Importance
A sound methodology ensures that the findings are valid, reliable, and reproducible. In [[critical-analysis]], evaluating the methodology is the most critical step in determining the strength of the research.
