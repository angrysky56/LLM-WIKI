# Scientific Writing

Scientific writing is a technical form of communication designed to report research findings clearly and concisely to a specialized audience. It is governed by conventions that ensure reproducibility and clarity.

## Structural Conventions (IMRaD)
Most scientific papers follow the **IMRaD** structure:
- **Introduction**: Why was the study done?
- **Methods**: How was it done?
- **Results**: What was found?
- **Discussion**: What does it mean?

## Key Principles
- **Precision**: Using exact terminology and quantitative data.
- **Objectivity**: Minimizing bias and focusing on observable evidence.
- **Reproducibility**: Providing enough detail in the Methods section for another researcher to duplicate the study.
- **Economy of Expression**: Removing redundant or vague language.
