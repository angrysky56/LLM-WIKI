# NEXUS

**NEXUS** (Neuro-Epistemic eXploration and Unified Synthesis Engine) is a cognitive architecture developed by [[tyler-hall|Ty]] that serves as an "operating system for cognition." It synthesizes neuroscience, control theory, and fractal geometry into a unified framework for intelligent systems.

## Core Architecture
- **Emotional Control Plane**: Uses the Russell Circumplex Model (Valence × Arousal) to dynamically adjust LLM hyperparameters.
- **PID Controller**: Regulates emotional transitions to prevent "state whiplash" and maintain cognitive stability.
- **Geometric Router (Artificial Corpus Callosum)**: Routes information between **Logic** and **Creative** manifolds based on intrinsic dimensionality.
- **Fractal Estimator (NFE)**: Estimates the Higuchi Fractal Dimension of activations, targeting the "Edge of Chaos" ($D_H \approx 1.8$) for optimal criticality.
- **Bicameral Engine**: A dual-manifold processing system that blends precision-focused logic with exploration-focused creativity.
- **Triune Brain Model**: Implements layers for Grammar/Structure (Reptilian), Context/Affect (Mammalian), and Abstract Reasoning (Neocortex), with a **4th Brain Synthesizer** for emergent integration.

## Technical Specs
- **Logic Manifold**: Low temperature, sparse activations, precision focus.
- **Creative Manifold**: High temperature, dense activations, exploration focus.
- **Dopamine Reward**: Implements Temporal Difference (TD) learning for prospective prediction rewards.
