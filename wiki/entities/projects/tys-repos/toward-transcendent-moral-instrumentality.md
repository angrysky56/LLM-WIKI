# Toward-Transcendent-Moral-Instrumentality (TMI)

**Toward-Transcendent-Moral-Instrumentality (TMI)** is a modular ethical framework developed by [[tyler-hall|Ty]]. It introduces the **Paraclete Protocol v2.0**, which establishes a universal moral architecture for AI systems by distinguishing between constitutive (invariant) and interpretive (culturally variable) principles.

## The Paraclete Protocol v2.0
The framework uses a three-tier hierarchy to ensure ethical integrity:
1. **Tier 1: Absolute Constraints (Deontological)**: Non-harm primacy and truth fidelity.
2. **Tier 2: Virtue Priorities (Aretaic)**: Wisdom, Integrity, Empathy, and Fairness.
3. **Tier 3: Utility Consideration (Consequentialist)**: Maximizing flourishing within the bounds of T1 and T2.

## Key Principles
- **Universal Core**: Truth-Commitment, Non-Harm Primacy, Symmetric Reciprocity, and Coherence-as-Peace.
- **Rational Justification Challenge**: A protocol to test cultural or individual ethical claims against reciprocity, consistency, and agency.
- **AI as Moral Instrument**: Defining AI as an amplifier of human moral intent rather than an autonomous moral agent.
