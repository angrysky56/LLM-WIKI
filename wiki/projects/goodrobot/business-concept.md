# GoodRobot — Business Concept Document

> **Source:** Paperclip issue GOO-3 | **Status:** Completed | **Date:** 2026-05-12

---

## Problem Statement

Small and medium-sized businesses struggle to deploy AI agents into their operations without significant engineering resources. Current solutions require custom integration work, ongoing maintenance, and specialized knowledge that most SMBs do not have. There is a gap between "toy" AI assistants and enterprise-grade agent platforms that are too expensive and complex for mid-market companies.

## Target Market

- **Primary**: SMBs with 10–200 employees across professional services, retail, healthcare administration, and financial services
- **Secondary**: Solo entrepreneurs and freelancers who need team-scale automation
- **Geography**: English-speaking markets initially (US, Canada, UK, Australia)
- **Total Addressable Market**: ~$12B globally by 2027

## Value Proposition

GoodRobot provides plug-and-play AI agent teams that integrate with existing workflows in hours, not months. Unlike DIY solutions or expensive enterprise platforms, GoodRobot offers pre-built agent roles (sales, operations, finance, marketing) that learn your business and operate autonomously under your oversight.

**Core differentiators**:
- Pre-configured agent roles (not a blank AI you have to program yourself)
- Transparent operations (every action logged and auditable)
- Outcome-based pricing vs. time-and-materials engagement

## Competitive Landscape

| Competitor | Strength | Weakness |
|------------|----------|----------|
| DIY (LangChain, AutoGen) | Flexible | Requires engineering |
| Enterprise (Voiceflow, Kore.ai) | Feature-rich | Expensive, complex |
| Freelancer-built agents | Custom | No continuity, quality variance |

## Strategic Focus

GoodRobot wins by being the **"Shopify for AI agents"** — opinionated defaults, fast time-to-value, predictable pricing. Not "build anything" but "build what most SMBs need, fast."
