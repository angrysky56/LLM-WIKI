# Time and Causal Networks (NKS)

Sections from Stephen Wolfram's *A New Kind of Science* (pages 488-489) discussing the representation of system evolution as networks of causal relationships.

## Summary

Wolfram explores a paradigm shift in representing the evolution of mobile automata. Instead of viewing evolution as a sequence of individual global steps (states), it can be viewed in terms of **updating events** and the **causal relationships** between them.

### The Causal Network Representation
- **Updating Events**: Discrete applications of the underlying rule, represented as nodes in a network.
- **Causal Connections**: "Stripes" of color representing information that carries from one updating event to the next, serving as the edges of the network.
- **Spacetime Model**: This network provides a fundamental model for spacetime, where time is not a global coordinate but a sequence of causal events.

### Visualization Sequence
The text describes a progression (Pictures a-i) that moves from the standard row-based representation to a highly regular network structure, emphasizing that the network itself contains all the essential information of the system's evolution.
